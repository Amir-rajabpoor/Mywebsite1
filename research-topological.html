Research

Topological phononic lattices

Mechanical lattices that support robust edge and interface modes, with a focus on one dimensional and ladder type structures inspired by Su Schrieffer Heeger models.

Overview

I work on mechanical and phononic realizations of topological lattices where geometry and coupling can be tuned to create band gaps and localized modes. These include single chains, coupled ladders, and structures with beyond nearest neighbor interactions.

  • Design of unit cells and coupling patterns to open and tune band gaps.
  • Analysis of edge and domain wall modes that live inside the band gaps.
  • Study of how long range couplings modify band topology and standard winding number calculations.

Related questions

  • How far the system can be perturbed while still supporting a useful interface mode.
  • How long range couplings change the correspondence between edge spectra and bulk invariants.
  • How fabrication tolerances and disorder affect localization and robustness.
