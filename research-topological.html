Research

Acoustic metamaterials with nonlocal interaction

Mechanical lattices that support robust edge and interface modes, with a focus on one dimensional and ladder type structures inspired by Su Schrieffer Heeger models. When nonlocal interactions are added, multiple edge states can arise even though the bulk winding number remains 1, revealing a breakdown of the standard winding number calculation, which fails to capture these additional modes.

Overview

I work on mechanical and phononic realizations of topological lattices where geometry and coupling can be tuned to create band gaps and localized modes. These include single chains, coupled ladders, and structures with beyond nearest neighbor interactions.

  • Design of unit cells and coupling patterns to open and tune band gaps.
  • Analysis of edge and domain wall modes that live inside the band gaps.
  • Study of how long range couplings modify band topology and standard winding number calculations.
Modes

Band gap, bulk, and interface modes

Below are representative clips from numerical and experimental work illustrating the difference between bulk and interface response in a topological phononic lattice with nonlocal interaction and a well defined band gap. Within the band gap, propagating waves are strongly attenuated, consistent with back scattering and the suppression of bulk transport. In contrast, a localized response can be intentionally engineered near the middle of the structure by introducing a topological mismatch between two regions, creating an interface state that concentrates energy at the designed domain wall.

Related questions

  • How far the system can be perturbed while still supporting a useful interface mode.
  • How long range couplings change the correspondence between edge spectra and bulk invariants.
  • How fabrication tolerances and disorder affect localization and robustness.
