Research

Nonlinear and amplitude dependent effects

It is important to understand how nonlinear interactions and finite amplitudes modify dispersion, localization, and energy transfer in mechanical and phononic lattices. In this research, the effect of nonlinearity in the nonlocal spring on wave propagation in a mass in mass chain has been studied.

Nonlinear mass in mass chains

I work with discrete chains that combine local resonators and nonlinear springs to study emerging wave phenomena and frequency shifts.

  • Models with both linear and nonlinear components in nearest neighbor springs.
  • Extension to chains with second neighbor interactions and internal resonators.
  • Response under wave packet excitation and periodic forcing.

Analytical approaches

Where possible I use perturbation and multiple scale methods to obtain approximate dispersion relations and amplitude frequency relationships.

  • Separation of fast oscillations and slow envelope evolution.
  • Derivation of effective equations for envelope dynamics.
  • Comparison between analytical predictions and numerical integration.

Role in applications

Understanding nonlinear effects is important for both design and reliability.

  • Predicting frequency shifts and bifurcations in small scale devices.
  • Controlling shock mitigation and energy absorption in architected materials.
  • Identifying operating ranges where nonlinearity is beneficial or harmful.
