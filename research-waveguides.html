Research

Waveguides and energy routing

Using band gaps, defects, and interface states to steer where energy travels in mechanical and acoustic structures.

Guided modes in phononic crystals

I study how patterned mechanical and acoustic media can be used to create channels where waves are guided while other directions are blocked.

  • Band gap design for targeted frequency ranges.
  • Defect lines and interfaces that act as effective waveguides.
  • Tradeoffs between confinement, loss, and fabrication simplicity.

Wave routing in perturbed lattices

In more complex lattices, I look at how local parameter changes, defects, or graded regions can route energy from one location to another.

  • Gradual versus abrupt transitions in mass or stiffness patterns.
  • Routing around defects or damage without losing too much energy.
  • Influence of damping and non ideal boundary conditions.

Simulation and verification

These problems are explored with a combination of analytical tools and numerical simulations.

  • Frequency domain and time domain simulations in COMSOL and custom codes.
  • Extraction of dispersion and group velocity from simulated wave packets.
  • Comparison with experiments where suitable samples are fabricated.
